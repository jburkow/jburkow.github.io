About Me

I am a 3rd year Ph.D. student in the Computational Mathematics, Science, and Engineering (CMSE) department at Michigan State University. I work under the mentorship of Dr. Adam Alessio as a member of the Medical Imaging & Data Integration (MIDI) Lab. I am interested in the real-world applications of deep learning in computer vision. My research focuses on rib fracture detection via deep convolutional neural networks on pediatric radiographs.

In my free time, I relax by watching movies (e.g., Marvel Cinematic Universe, Lord of the Rings, Galaxy Quest, Deadpool) and TV shows (e.g., Castle, Psych, Brooklyn Nine-Nine, Arrow, The Flash, The Blacklist), drumming along to symphonic/power/progressive metal (e.g., Epica, Kamelot, Dream Theater, Symphony X), and playing video games (e.g., StarCraft II, Mass Effect, Dragon Age, CounterStrike: Global Offensive). Prior to the Covid-19 pandemic, I enjoyed traveling and hiking with my wonderful girlfriend, Tiffany.
